It is called from the previously analyzed OCL program (AR901P.ocl36.txt). Below, I’ll explain the process steps, business rules, tables (files) used, and external programs called.

    Business Rules¶

    The program enforces the following business rules for generating the customer master listing:

    1. Company Selection (kyalco):
    2. Must be 'ALL' (all companies) or 'CO ' (specific companies).

    3. If 'CO ', at least one valid company number (kyco1, kyco2, or kyco3) must be provided and must exist in arcont without being deleted (acdel ≠ 'D').

    4. Customer Selection (kyalcs):

    5. Must be 'ALL' (all customers) or 'SEL' (selected customers).

    6. If 'SEL', at least one customer number (kycs01 to kycs10) must be non-zero.

    7. Address and Dollar List Flags (addlst, dollst):

    8. Must be 'Y' (yes), 'N' (no), or blank.

    9. If both are not 'Y', addlst defaults to 'Y' to ensure at least one report type is generated.

    10. Job Queue Flag (kyjobq):

    11. Must be 'Y' (queue the job), 'N' (run interactively), or blank.

    12. Number of Copies (kycopy):

    13. Defaults to 1 if zero is entered.

    14. Data Validation:

    15. Company numbers must exist in arcont and not be marked as deleted.

    16. Invalid inputs result in error messages displayed to the user, prompting correction.

    17. Initialization:

    18. The onetim subroutine populates default values and retrieves up to three company records from arcont for display.
    19. The gscont file provides a default company number if available; otherwise, 'ALL' is used.
